				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><em>DistillerSR Customers Can Now Search for and Purchase Content Directly from CCC’s Proprietary Bibliographic Database of More Than 165 Million Articles</em></p>
<p><strong>San Diego, California, February 20 2025</strong> – DistillerSR® Inc., the market leader in literature review automation software and creator of DistillerSR®, today announced an agreement to expand its collaboration with <a href="http://www.copyright.com/">CCC</a> so that DistillerSR users can submit orders for scholarly journal articles directly from CCC’s <a href="https://www.copyright.com/solutions-rightfind/">RightFind Enterprise</a> software solution. Available today, this integration saves time for users by eliminating the need for systematic review teams to log into multiple systems and websites to order, pay for, and reconcile payment records for non-subscribed scientific content. Both companies will co-present this integration at the company’s <a href="https://www.distillersr.com/evidence-matters/evidence-matters-2025">Evidence Matters</a> Summit user group meeting in San Diego on February 20th.</p>
<p>“DistillerSR is excited to be partnering with CCC to automate the procurement of scientific references required for our user community,” said Peter O’Blenis, CEO, DistillerSR Inc. “The timely access to and purchase of RightFind content will help researchers to reduce even further the time to process literature and synthesize evidence for their reviews, resulting in lower review costs and faster time to market for medical products and healthcare guidelines.”</p>
<p>RightFind Enterprise is part of <a href="https://www.copyright.com/solutions-rightfind-suite/">CCC’s RightFind Suite</a>, a set of integrated, flexible software modules that enable professionals at R&amp;D-intensive companies to search, discover, access, read, share, organize, collaborate on, and analyze content and generate data-driven insights. The new integration between RightFind Enterprise and DistillerSR builds upon the existing integration between the two systems, which enables DistillerSR users to search RightFind Enterprise’s bibliographic database for content, while leveraging DistillerSR’s validated workflow to reduce literature review times by as much as 70%.</p>
<p>“Finding relevant, trusted information and staying on top of the latest research is mission-critical work,” said Lauren Tulloch, Vice President and Managing Director, CCC. “The expanded integration of RightFind Enterprise with DistillerSR makes it easier for researchers to immediately acquire the content they need, aiding companies in their pursuit to speed up innovation.”</p>
<p>CCC helps companies manage vast amounts of published content and proprietary internal information and data. As experts in processing data from thousands of sources across diverse industries, CCC has established a proven record of collaborating with many companies, including content providers and technology partners, to unify data sources and make content more discoverable.</p>
<p>DistillerSR’s evidence management platform enables customers to securely automate the collection, re-use, and analysis of literature review research—faster, more accurately, and more transparently at scale. DistillerSR is a pioneer in literature reviews. Since 2016, customers have trusted DistillerSR to accelerate the completion and enhance the quality of the literature reviews. DistillerSR enables reviewers, in real-time, to evaluate suggested answers and effortlessly identify the source text, ensuring greater levels of traceability and validation of a reviewer’s selected evidence for regulatory submissions.</p>
<p>Today, more than 260 of the world’s leading research organizations, including most of the largest pharmaceutical and medical device companies, trust DistillerSR to securely produce transparent, audit-ready, and regulatory compliant literature reviews. With more organizations using DistillerSR to automate their literature reviews, healthcare researchers can make more informed and time-sensitive health policy decisions, clinical practice guidelines, and regulatory submissions, while delivering better overall research.</p>
